A timeless dessert with flaky crust and sweet cinnamon-spiced apples — just like Grandma made!

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
  • 6–7 tbsp ice water
  • 6 cups thinly sliced apples (Granny Smith or Honeycrisp)
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine flour and salt. Cut in butter until mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Stir in ice water, a tablespoon at a time, until dough forms. Divide in half and chill for 30 minutes.
  2. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
  3. In another bowl, toss apples with sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice.
  4. Roll out one half of dough and fit into a 9-inch pie plate. Fill with apple mixture and dot with butter.
  5. Roll out remaining dough and place over filling. Seal and flute edges. Cut slits in top crust for steam to escape.
  6. Brush crust with beaten egg for a golden finish.
  7. Bake for 45–50 minutes, until crust is golden brown and filling is bubbly. Cool before serving.

Tips and Tricks

Serve warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for that Pioneer Woman touch.
